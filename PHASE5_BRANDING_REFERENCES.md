## Inspiration · pop-art / bold-outline (operator, 2026-04-19)

A reference image was shared during Phase 1 build with the note "would lend itself well to the niche". Tabled per scope discipline.

**Style characteristics:**
- Saturated 3-colour palette: hot pink, electric cyan/teal, chrome yellow — anchored on white/cream cards with thick black outlines
- Chunky black sans-serif display type (think *condensed grotesque*, all-caps, heavy weight) for headings
- Cards as pop-art "stickers": 4–6px black border, square or near-square corners, blocky drop in front of a flat coloured background
- Iconography rendered as flat illustrated tiles in the same palette (not line icons, not photos) — each tile sits in its own rounded square of one of the three accent colours
- Phone/browser-frame chrome treated as a graphic element, not skeuomorphic
- Single primary CTA per surface (`VIEW CART`-style), full-width, in one of the accent colours

**Why it fits the niche:**
- Pokémon TCG audience already lives in saturated art and bold rarity badges — a maximalist style reads as native, not loud
- The buylist flow has very few primary actions (search, add, submit, get paid) — a high-contrast palette makes those legible without hierarchy guesswork
- Pop-art tiles map naturally onto the existing card-grid IA from Phase 1

**Risks to manage when applying:**
- Three saturated accents + black outlines can compete with the actual card art when shown side-by-side. Test `/card/[id]` early.
- Heavy display type at small sizes (table headers, admin labels) will need a quieter secondary face
- Accessibility: confirm AA contrast on every accent-on-accent pairing before locking the palette
