Lewis's Pokémon Platform 03 · Two flows
The architecture

Two flows. One catalogue.

Buy-in and sell-out share one spine — cb_cards. Every Charizard on the site is the same Charizard. No re-entry, no duplicate listings, no price drift.

A · Buylist

They sell to you.

Customer builds a submission, sees an instant offer, posts cards in, gets paid on verify.

  • Instant GBP offer — no reply window
  • Royal Mail label generated as PDF
  • Payout by PayPal or +10% trade credit
  • Reference · CB-2026-000042
B · Shopfront

You sell to them.

You list stock per unit with photos. Customer browses, baskets, checks out with Stripe.

  • Per-listing price (every unit has its SKU)
  • Condition & grade on every card
  • Featured rail you curate by hand
  • Apple Pay · Google Pay · card

The sell-in flow

Five minutes. Zero emails.

The instant-offer experience is what earns the repeat customer. Everything else is scaffolding around it.

01

Search

Type a card name. Autocomplete lists every variant in every set.

02

Add

Pick condition. Card joins a running submission with live total.

03

Offer

Total offer appears — cash or +10% trade credit. Frozen for 7 days on accept.

04

Ship

Royal Mail label emailed as PDF. Tracking auto-updates status.

05

Paid

You verify. One click. PayPal Payouts does the rest.

Worked example · the Gyarados Lewis just bought.

Aisha lists her old Base-set Gyarados at Near Mint. The formula returns £26.40. She accepts; the offer freezes for seven days; her Royal Mail label appears in her inbox. Card posted Monday — PayPal pings her Thursday. No email tennis, no haggling.

Average end-to-end: card posted Monday, seller paid Thursday. Both sides like it that way.

Lewis's Pokémon Platform 06 · Data engine
The hard bit · done cheap

We only rent what moves.

Card names don't change. Prices do. So the catalogue is stored once, on your own infrastructure — and we only pay for feeds that genuinely update. No recurring subscription where there doesn't need to be one.

Source · 01

Self-hosted catalogue

The deliberate saving. A quarterly snapshot of the public card catalogue lives in your own Supabase. One-time ingest. Zero ongoing API cost.

Free · 02

TCGCSV

A free daily mirror of TCGplayer's live market data. Low · mid · high · market · direct-low — all conditions, all printings. Pulled nightly. £0 per month.

Free · 03

open.er-api.com

No-cost FX. Daily USD → GBP rates, stamped with every transaction. Free tier is stable and audit-friendly.

Why this matters: a typical off-the-shelf Pokémon platform pays £30–£400 a month for catalogue and price APIs. Yours pays £0 — catalogue lives locally, prices come from a free daily TCGplayer mirror, FX is free. No computed offers are stored; raw inputs go in, the offer is recalculated against your live margins on every view. Move a dial at 9am — every offer on the site updates by 9:01.

Lifecycle
